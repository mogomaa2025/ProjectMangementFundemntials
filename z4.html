1.4 The Role of the Project Manager

Project managers play a pivotal role in bringing projects to successful completion. They serve as the central coordination point, ensuring all elements work together harmoniously[211][212].

Core Project Manager Responsibilities:

The project managers must:

Stakeholder Collaboration

Work closely with other stakeholders on a project, especially the sponsor and project team

Knowledge Areas Mastery

Be familiar with the 10 project management knowledge areas and understand their interconnections

Tools & Techniques

Know various tools and techniques related to project management and when to apply them effectively

Performance Monitoring

Track project progress, identify issues early, and implement corrective actions to keep projects on track

Resource Management

Allocate and optimize resources efficiently while managing budgets, timelines, and quality expectations

Communication Hub

Act as the primary communication point between team members, stakeholders, and organizational leadership

Stakeholder Collaboration Excellence

Project managers must excel at stakeholder management, as they serve as the central coordination point for all project participants[213][216]. This includes managing relationships with sponsors who provide direction and funding, team members who execute the work, and external stakeholders who influence or are impacted by the project outcomes.

1.4B Suggested Skills for Project Managers

A 2013 survey by CIO.com listed seven skills project managers need in order to be effective and successful in leading projects. These skills remain highly relevant today and form the foundation for project management excellence[220][221].

1. Be Highly Organized

Project managers must excel at organizing complex information, managing multiple tasks simultaneously, and maintaining clear documentation. Strong organizational skills enable effective resource coordination and timeline management[211].

2. Take Charge and Know How to Lead

Effective leadership involves inspiring teams, making decisive decisions, and guiding projects through challenges. Project managers must demonstrate authority while fostering collaboration and team engagement[214].

3. Be an Effective Communicator

Clear, consistent communication is essential for stakeholder alignment, team coordination, and project success. This includes both verbal and written communication across various audiences and contexts[212].

4. Know How and When to Negotiate

Project managers must negotiate resources, timelines, scope changes, and conflict resolution. Strong negotiation skills help balance competing interests and achieve win-win outcomes for all stakeholders.

5. Be Detail-Oriented

Attention to detail ensures project requirements are met, risks are identified early, and quality standards are maintained. Small oversights can lead to significant project problems and failures.

6. Recognize and Solve Problems Quickly

Projects inevitably encounter unexpected challenges. Successful project managers can quickly identify issues, analyze root causes, and implement effective solutions to keep projects on track[214].

7. Possess the Necessary Technical Skills

Project managers need relevant technical knowledge in their industry, familiarity with project management tools and software, and understanding of the technologies involved in their projects.

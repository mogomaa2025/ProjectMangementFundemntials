Core Project Management Concepts

What is a Project?

A project is a temporary endeavor undertaken to create a unique product, service, or result. Projects are unique, temporary and developed incrementally; They require resources, have a sponsor, and involve uncertainty.

The Triple Constraint

The triple constraint of project management refers to managing the scope, time and cost dimensions of a project. These three elements must be carefully balanced to ensure project success.

Project Management

Project management is the application of knowledge, skills, tools and techniques to project activities to meet project requirements. It encompasses a comprehensive framework for successful project delivery.

PM Framework

A framework for project management includes the project stakeholders, project management knowledge areas, and project management tools and techniques. These elements work together systematically.

Programs

A program is a group of related projects, subprograms, and program activities managed in a coordinated way to obtain benefits. Programs deliver value that individual projects cannot achieve alone.

Portfolio Management

Project portfolio management involves organizing and managing projects and programs that contribute to the entire enterprise's success. It ensures strategic alignment and maximum business value.
